The width of each connection (or "link") is proportional to the quantity it represents, making it an effective tool for understanding distributions, processes, and inefficiencies in a system.

Originally developed for energy efficiency analysis, Sankey diagrams are now widely used across industries for **data visualization** to help users track flow, identify bottlenecks, and optimize processes.
